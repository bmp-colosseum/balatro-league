# Reporting, Confirming & Disputes

How a result becomes official, and what to do when something goes wrong.

---

## Reporting a result

Two paths, same outcome:

- **Guided (`/start-match`)** — if you played through the bot, the result is logged
  automatically when both of you vote the winner of each game. Nothing else to do.
- **Manual (`/report @opponent result:2-0`)** — if you played in BMP without the
  bot, the **winner (or either player) reports the score**: `2-0`, `1-1`, or `0-2`.

Results post to the league's results channel, and to `/standings` on the site.

## Confirming

When a result is reported manually, your **opponent gets a confirm/dispute prompt**.

- They **confirm** → it's official and counts in the standings.
- If they do nothing, it **auto-confirms** after a set window (so a result can't be
  held hostage by someone ignoring it).
- They **dispute** → see below.

> Tip: always glance at the score posted for your match and make sure it's right —
> confirming is how the league knows it's correct.

## Disputes

If a reported score is wrong, hit **Dispute** (in Discord, or from your profile on
the site) and say what it should be. A **helper/admin** is pinged to review and
sets the correct result. Disputed matches show a badge until resolved.

---

## Disconnects

If someone disconnects mid-game, use the **"Opponent DC'd"** button in the guided
match. The disconnecting player then either **confirms** (they forfeit that game) or
says they're **still here** (you keep playing). If it can't be sorted out, call a
helper — staff make the final call.

(The exact "before vs. after a certain point in the game" rule is at the admins'
discretion; when in doubt, grab a helper with `/helper`.)

---

## Forfeits & DQs

An admin can award a result **by DQ** — a `2-0` given without playing it out — for
things like a no-show, a drop-out, or a rule violation. On the site these show as
**"W by DQ"**; the reason is admin-only and not shown to other players.

If you can't make a match work (scheduling, an unresponsive opponent), reach out to
a helper/admin **before** the deadline rather than just not playing — that's what
keeps the season fair.

---

## Getting help

- **`/helper`** in Discord summons a helper to your match.
- Helpers can join your match channel, mediate, and record a verbally-agreed result.
- Anything bigger (bans, repeat no-shows) is handled by the admins case-by-case.
